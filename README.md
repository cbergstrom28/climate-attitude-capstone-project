# climate-attitude-capstone-project
Main README explains project question(s), rationale, audience, data sources, and process (metacode).
I wanted to understand if people's attitudes about climate change alter after major climate-related disasters. I used the Climate change and the American Mind survey (CCAM) from the Yale Program on Climate Communication and the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA)'s billion dollar disaster report, an annual data analysis that provides data on disaster events that cost more than $1 billion USD. 

I intend for this data to be used by scientists studying climate change who want to communicate the impact climate-related disasters are having on people's attitudes across the US. Understanding how attitudes about climate change shift after costly and deadly hurricanes occur can help climate scientists better understand public sentiment while communicating their research. We can see people have grown more concerned about climate change as time as gone on and that there are significant increase after 2017, where we saw the most expensive disaster in the United States, Hurricane Harvey, take place. Showing that climate change is not a distant problem can help scientists better argue for funding for research that can help us mitigate and adapt to a changing climate.
